# Saboteur

> *Bait the coil. Wait for the foot.*

You are the Heretic's trapper. Each night, you place a trap on one operative. If the Loyalists target them — Interrogation, Mentat, Liturgy, even a kill — the next action against that operative *fails*, the actor pays an extra drift cost, and the cell is told nothing happened.

**Night action — Trap.** One target. The next action against them fizzles, the actor pays an extra +5 drift on top of their chosen tier.

**Drift cost:** +1 per night you trap.

**Saboteur intel:** You learn *that* a trap sprung and *who* the actor was. You do not learn what they tried to do.

**Saboteur traps do not stack.** One trap per operative per night. Trap on a player, the player is safe until the trap springs.

**Play this role if:** you want to *punish* the Loyalists for acting — make every Interrogator think twice about scanning, every Chirurgeon hesitate.

**Loyalists should look for:** the trap fires when *someone targets the trapped player*. If a T2 Interrogator suddenly pays +5 drift for "nothing happened," you have a trap. The trap-target is *not* the trapped player — it is the *actor*. Use this to invert: scan who paid the +5.

**Heretics should look for:** traps land on Interrogators. Trap the Interrogator's favorite target and the next Interrogation scan costs +7 total drift. They start to *fear* acting.
