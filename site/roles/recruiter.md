# Recruiter

> *They came to you clean. You left them made.*

You are the Heretic's recruiter. Each night, you perform the *catalyst* — a ritual attempt to convert one operative into a Heretic. The catalyst *only* takes hold if the target is at maximum drift (Black zone, drift = 20).

**Night action — Catalyst.** One target. If their drift is at the maximum, they become a Heretic immediately. If they are *not* at max, the catalyst fails silently. You drift either way.

**Drift cost:** +3 per night you catalyze. The single most expensive night action in v1.

**Why you're expensive:** the Recruiter's whole point is *only* a Black-zone target. Lesser targets mean wasted drift. Save the catalyst for the right moment.

**What you learn:** nothing. The system tells you only "the catalyst takes hold" on success; failures are silent.

**Play this role if:** you want to *flip the parity math*. One conversion swings living Heretics by +1 and living Loyalists by −1. Two conversions win the game.

**Loyalists should look for:** the Recruiter *cannot act on a clean target*. Watch who climbs into Orange, Red, and Black — and watch who stops climbing at Black (they were either lynched or converted). The gap between Black entries and Black exits is your Recruiter cadence.

**Heretics should look for:** a Heretic Priest + Recruiter pair is *the* late-game combo. The Priest pushes a Loyalist to Black; the Recruiter converts them; the cell gains a member. They go from minority to majority in three rounds.
