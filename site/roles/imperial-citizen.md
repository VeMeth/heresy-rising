# Imperial Citizen

> *The Throne does not require you to be mighty. Only to be true.*

You have no night action. You cannot interrogate, protect, divine, or sermonize. What you have is a vote, two eyes, and the loyalty already written into your chest.

The Citadel is full of people exactly like you — that is its strength and its danger. Eleven conclaves, ten of them won by who noticed first.

**Night action:** None. Sleep if you wish.

**Drift cost (Warp-corruption on your soul):** Zero. You climb drift only by witnessing violence, by voting with the losing side, or by being on the wrong end of a Heretic Priest's sermon.

**Play this role if:** you want to be the Loyalist backbone — the one whose votes and observations tip the day.

**Loyalists should look for:** Citizens are the tell. A Heretic cell rotates its night actions between Mine, Trap, Sermon, Catalyst, Forgery — *all* of those who don't match a Citizen claim. If someone claims Citizen and you find them posting nightly, that is a confession.

**Heretics should look for:** Citizens are targets of opportunity — no power, no protection, no special value to the cell, *and* they vote. Killing one each night slowly strangles the day-game.
