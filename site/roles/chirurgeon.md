# Chirurgeon

> *Cut what needs cutting. Close what needs closing.*

You are the Loyalist healer. Each night, you designate one operative to protect — *yourself included*. If they are killed, crippled, or otherwise struck by the night, the strike fails silently. **You never learn whether your protection actually fired.** You learn only that you used your night action.

**Night action — Protect.** One target (self is allowed). Their protection-blocks kill **and** cripple that night, silently.

**Drift cost (Warp invoice):** +1 per night you protect.

## The rotation rule

You may not protect the same operative two nights in a row. **This includes yourself.** The Conclave is too small, the rotation too visible, the Heretics too attentive — if you turtle on a single person (or yourself) night after night, the cell knows who you are *and* who you're shielding.

Practically: self-protection is a *once every other night* mechanic, not a daily shield. The Chirurgeon who self-protects every night is a Heretic target, not a healthy operator.

## The information asymmetry (and why it matters)

The Chirurgeon **does not learn** whether protection fired. The Arbitrator **does**. If you are the cell's only defender, you will spend nights wondering whether your read was right. This is the cost of being invisible — *and* it is what makes you useful. A defender who doesn't know their own success rate cannot be read off their reactions.

When the day reveals a player who should be dead but isn't, you now know your protection fired. **Keep that knowledge to yourself.** Announcing it confirms your role to any Heretic who's listening.

## Coverage rules

- **Kill blocked:** Murderer acts on your target → kill fails, Murderer wastes their action, you never know.
- **Cripple blocked:** Recruiter's heretical-catalyst (or future Saboteur traps that cripple) on your target → cripple fails silently.
- **Both defenders on same target:** if you *and* the Arbitrator protect the same target, protection is *redundant*, not doubled. (See [Arbitrator](/roles/arbitrator).) Don't stack defenders — coordinate so one protects the Interrogator and the other protects the Priest.
- **Day-phase Interrogation is not a night strike.** You cannot save someone from a T3 Interrogation.

## Play this role if

You want to be the cell's insurance policy, you are comfortable acting without feedback, and you are willing to take T1 drift every round you protect. The Chirurgeon's power is **silence and reliability** — your value is invisible until the day someone who should be dead is alive.

## Loyalists should look for

A Chirurgeon who is acting nightly but **never reading publicly about their own success rate** is exhibiting exactly the right behavior. A defender who says *"I saved X last night"* is either bluffing or has already lost their anonymity.

## Heretics should look for

If a player is *never* dying despite being publicly named — they are defended. The defender is a T1 role. Find who is also at low drift and *never* seen acting, and you may have your Chirurgeon. Then kill them *while* they're not protecting — and you don't even need to test, because the rotation rule will tell you.
