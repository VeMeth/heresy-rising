# Arbitrator

> *Stand between the blade and the throat. Pay the price if you must.*

You are the Loyalist bodyguard — the *loud* one. Each night, you designate one operative as your proxy. If a night-kill strikes them, **the kill redirects to you.** You die. They live. Your death is announced publicly the next day: *"X was slain in the night, taking the strike meant for Y."*

This is your power and your cost: **protection by martyrdom.**

**Night action — Guard (bodyguard-proxy).** One target. If they are struck, *you* take the killing blow and they survive.

**Drift cost (Warp invoice):** +1 per night you guard. Same as the Chirurgeon.

**Self-targeting:** ❌ **You may not guard yourself.** This is the one rule every Arbitrator knows by heart. Self-proxy would make the sacrifice meaningless.

**Rotation rule:** you cannot proxy the same player on two consecutive nights (mirrors the Chirurgeon's rotation rule). Re-rotate through your candidates — the Loyalists you want to protect change night by night.

**Stacking with the Chirurgeon:** if both you and a Chirurgeon protect the same target the same night, **you proxy first and die**. The Chirurgeon's protect is then a no-op (the target was already saved by your sacrifice). The cell ends up with one fewer defender but the same target alive. The "very sad arbitrator."

**When you are crippled** (cripple tier > 0), the engine silently rejects your action — no error, no drift cost. You sit the night out. Mirrors Chirurgeon behavior.

**Play this role if:** you want to absorb aggression and force the Murderer to overextend. Your loud death is *also* a tell — once you've died, the cell knows there was a proxy that night.

**Loyalists should look for:** the public system message names the Arbitrator who died and the strike's intended target. The target is worth protecting again next time the Murderer moves on them.

**Heretics should look for:** an Arbitrator who keeps proxying the same target is a free double-kill opportunity — strike *the Arbitrator themselves*. If you kill an Arbitrator and the next message is *not* their death, you killed a defender with no proxy that night (and the target lives regardless). Either way, you removed the cell's loudest sacrifice.
