# Sanctioned Psyker

> *The Astronomican burns through your skull. You were told this was a gift.*

You are a sanctioned psyker of the Imperium — licensed, registered, soul-bound to the Emperor's light. The Inquisition knows what you are. What they don't know is what you are *capable of*. You carry a warp-channel inside you, and on one night of your choosing, you can tear it open and kill.

**Night action — Warp-kill.** One target, once per game. No night-action limit beyond that single use.

| Action | Effect (target) | Cost (you) | Uses |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Warp-kill** | Target dies | **+15 drift** (lands you at Red zone) | Once per game |

**The cost is the kill.** When you fire, your drift jumps to **Red zone (15–19)** in a single night. From that moment on, you are a walking time bomb:

- Any **T2+ Interrogator** scan against you triggers **Execute on Sight** — you die from your own side's strongest weapon.
- The cell has to decide what to do with you. Lynch you preemptively? Hide you behind a credible claim? Leave you at the table and pray the Interrogator doesn't look?
- A crippled Psyker (T1+) **cannot fire the kill** that round. T2/T3 lockouts are permanent — the kill is gone for the rest of the game.

**No claim.** You look identical to the Murderer from the table's perspective. Same drift curve, same lack of public marker, same night-only presence. The Loyalist Interrogator cannot distinguish you from a Heretic through any public signal — they have to read drift deltas and reason about who's who. **That ambiguity is your protection.**

**Interrupts mirror the Murderer:**
- A **Chirurgeon** protect on your target silently blocks the kill. The kill is consumed (single-use), and the drift cost still hits you.
- An **Arbitrator** bodyguard on your target redirects the kill to the Arbitrator. They die. You still drift to Red.
- A **Saboteur** trap on you adds +5 drift on top of the +15 (lands you at Black — drift overflow auto-converts you). The kill is **not** consumed; you can still fire it on a later night if you survive.

**Play this role if:** you want to be the Loyalist's *one-shot trump card*. Pick the right moment. Hide your drift climb until you fire. Manage your post-fire survival.

**Loyalists should look for:** a player at Red zone who *isn't* a confirmed Heretic and *wasn't* at Orange the day before. That's the Psyker signature. Don't scan them — coordinate the Interrogator elsewhere. If they fire and the body count matches the kill, you've found them.

**Heretics should look for:** the Psyker's drift climbs by **+15 in one night**. That's a much sharper jump than any other role. Watch the public drift-zone transitions; a Red-zone player who appeared clean the day before is almost certainly the Psyker post-fire. Lynch them before the cell figures out what to do with them.

**Cripple handling:**
- T1 cripple on the night you wanted to fire → kill blocked **for that round**. You keep the kill and can fire on a later night (provided T1 recovers).
- T2 or T3 cripple → kill blocked **for the rest of the game**. You become a non-functional Loyalist with a Red-zone drift meter; the cell will likely lynch you.

**Composition:**
- Ships at **≥7 players**. Below 7p, the cell is too small — the Interrogator scan is too obvious and the Psyker becomes a guaranteed self-kill.
- **Max 1 per game.** Multiple sanctioned psykers is too much Warp in the conclave.
- **Optional.** Cells at 7p+ may include the role. Composition table: see `data/composition.json`.
