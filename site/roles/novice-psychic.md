# Novice-Psychic

> *You feel the warp as a pressure on the eyes. You cannot shape it. You can only witness.*

You are the Loyalist seer. Each night, the warp grants you a glimpse of one operative's drift. You receive a *qualitative hint* — a sensation, an impression — but no number, no zone, no faction.

**Night action — Drift Hint.** One target. The system sends you a subjective whisper about their drift zone.

**Drift cost (Warp invoice):** +1 per night you hint.

**Accuracy:** Hints are gated by the *target's* drift zone — clean targets give exact hints, drifted targets give noisy hints, and very-drifted targets give *very noisy* hints. You will usually be right; sometimes you will not.

**You do not learn:** the target's role, faction, drift number, or night action. You learn *how drifted they feel*.

**Play this role if:** you want to be the Loyalist's most reliable tell, in exchange for never being able to *confirm* anything.

**Loyalists should look for:** the Novice-Psychic's hints are *qualitative* — they describe the target's drift, not their faction. A hint of *something clean* on a publicly named Heretic is gold. A hint of *something terrible* on a publicly named Loyalist is a reprieve.

**Heretics should look for:** the Novice-Psychic reads you. If your drift climbs past Yellow, they read you fuzzier. If you stay in Green, they read you exactly. The fix: a Heretic Priest sermon can push a *Loyalist target* into Orange, which the seer then misreads as a Heretic.
