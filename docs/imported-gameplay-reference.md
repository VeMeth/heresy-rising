## 1. Overview

5-Minute Dungeon is a **fully cooperative, real-time card game**. There are no turns. All players act simultaneously, racing against a 5-minute countdown clock to defeat a series of Door Cards (monsters, obstacles, and people) followed by a final Boss. In the **base game**, you win by clearing Bosses #1–#5 in order. With the **Kickstarter Booster / Big Box expanded content**, Bosses #6–#7 are added after the base-game bosses.

The core loop: flip a Dungeon Card → play cards from your hand to match the symbols on it → sweep it away → flip the next card. Keep going until the Boss is defeated or the clock hits zero.

## 2. Components

This guide treats the **base retail game** as the default ruleset, then clearly marks extra material from the **Kickstarter Booster**, **Curses! Foiled Again!**, and **Big Box / combined editions**.

### 2a. Base Game

**Base Game (250 cards total)**

- 5 double-sided Hero Mats (10 heroes, one per side)
- 5 Hero Decks of 40 cards each (one per mat, color-coded)
- 5 Boss Mats (#1–#5)
- 40 Door Cards (monsters, obstacles, people)
- 10 Challenge Cards (5 mini-bosses, 5 events)
- Rulebook

**Base-game win condition:** defeat Bosses #1–#5 in order, ending with **The Dungeon Master**.

### 2b. Kickstarter Booster / Expanded Bosses

The Kickstarter Booster adds:

- Boss #6 — **The K.I.C.K. 9000**
- Boss #7 — **The Dungeon Master (Final Form)**
- Extra Door Cards
- Extra Challenge Cards

**Expanded win condition:** defeat Bosses #1–#7 in order, ending with **The Dungeon Master (Final Form)**.

### 2c. Curses! Foiled Again! Expansion

The Curses! Foiled Again! expansion adds additional systems rather than just more normal dungeon cards:

- Druid / Shaman hero mat and deck
- Boss Cards for bosses
- Curse cards, which remain active until removed
- Artifact cards
- New Action Cards added to the original hero decks
- Support for a 6th player

When using Curses!, each boss dungeon starts with that boss's Boss Cards, then enough Door Cards are added to reach the total printed on the Boss Mat, then Challenge Cards are added as normal.

### 2d. Big Box / Combined Editions

The Big Box combines the base game, Curses! Foiled Again!, and the Kickstarter Booster. In practice, this means it contains the 7-boss expanded campaign and the Curses/Artifacts/Boss Cards module.

**Not included:** A timer. Use the free official timer app (see Section 6) or any phone timer.

## 3. The Five Symbols

Every resource card and dungeon card uses one or more of these five symbols:

| Symbol | Color | Description |
|--------|-------|-------------|
| **Sword** | Red | Melee attack |
| **Arrow** | Green | Ranged attack |
| **Scroll** | Blue | Magic/arcane |
| **Jump** (Running figure) | Purple | Acrobatics/evasion |
| **Shield** | Yellow | Defense/protection |

Matching symbols from your hand to the symbols on a Dungeon Card is the primary way to defeat it.

## 4. Card Types Explained

### 4a. Resource Cards (colored border matching deck color)

The backbone of every hero deck. Each card has one or more large symbols on it. You play these face-up to the center of the table to match the symbols on an active Dungeon Card.

- **Single-symbol cards** — one large symbol; most common.
- **Double-symbol cards** — show two symbols. You do not have to use both. They can serve as either, or both when a card needs both. Most prevalent in the Barbarian/Gladiator (Red) deck (e.g., a Sword + another symbol).

### 4b. Action Cards (black border)

Unique to each Hero Deck. They perform an effect directly rather than contributing symbols. Key action cards:

| Card | Effect | Found In |
|------|--------|---------|
| **Fireball** | Instantly defeat a Monster | Blue (Wizard/Sorceress) |
| **Magic Bomb** | Counts as 1 of each symbol when played | Blue |
| **Cancel** | Prevents an Event Card from taking effect | Blue |
| **Holy Hand Grenade** | Instantly defeats ANY card — Monster, Obstacle, Person, Mini-Boss, Event, or Boss | Yellow (Paladin/Valkyrie) |
| **Divine Shield** | Pauses the timer; all players draw 1 card | Yellow |
| **Heal** | One player puts their entire discard pile back into their draw pile | Yellow |
| **Health Potion** | All players recover 3 cards from their discard pile to hand | Yellow |
| **Enrage** | Choose any 2 players — each draws 3 cards | Red (Barbarian/Gladiator) |
| **Mighty Leap** | Instantly defeat an Obstacle | Red |
| **Donate** | Give your entire current hand to another player; you draw a new hand | Purple (Ninja/Thief) |
| **Steal** | Take another player's entire hand; they draw a new hand | Purple |
| **Backstab** | Instantly defeat a Person | Purple |
| **Sprint** | Instantly defeat an Obstacle | Purple |
| **Snipe** | Instantly defeat a Person | Green (Ranger/Huntress) |
| **Wild Card** | Can be played as any 1 symbol of your choice | Green |
| **Healing Herbs** | One player recovers 4 cards from their discard pile to hand | Green |

> **Note:** Fireball, Backstab, Snipe, Mighty Leap, and similar "defeat a [type]" action cards **cannot** defeat Mini-Bosses or Bosses. The Holy Hand Grenade is the sole exception.

### 4c. Door Cards (dungeon back)

40 in the base game. Each has:
- **A flavor description** of the threat (e.g., "A Cactus That Wants a Hug," "Squire Nedward")
- **A set of symbols** (1–4 total) that must be matched to defeat it
- **A type:** Monster, Obstacle, or Person

### 4d. Challenge Cards (horned-skull back)

Two sub-types, mixed into the dungeon deck. There are 2 Challenge Cards per player per dungeon.

**Mini-Bosses** — Tougher than regular Door Cards. More symbols required. They have NO type (not Monster, Obstacle, or Person), so Hero Abilities and type-specific Action Cards (Fireball, Backstab, etc.) cannot defeat them. Only resource-matching or the Holy Hand Grenade works.

**Event Cards** — Identified by a "^" (caret/hat) icon. When flipped, the team must immediately perform whatever the card says before the next card can be flipped. Cancel or Holy Hand Grenade can prevent the event from taking effect.

### 4e. Boss Mats

Not cards in the dungeon deck; they sit under the dungeon pile. When the entire dungeon pile is cleared, the Boss Mat is revealed and the team must match its symbols to win.

## 5. Setup

### Step-by-Step Setup

**1. Choose Heroes**
Each player picks one hero and places their Hero Mat face-up in front of them (choose which side — e.g., Wizard or Sorceress). No two players can use the same mat (same color deck).

**2. Get Your Deck**
Each player takes the deck matching their mat's color. Shuffle it and place it face-down on the "Draw Pile" space on your Hero Mat.

**3. Two-Player Rule**
In a 2-player game, each player also selects a second (unused) hero deck and shuffles it into their own deck. This gives each player access to more cards since the hand sizes are smaller as well.

**4. Draw Your Starting Hand**

| # Players | Starting Hand Size |
|-----------|-------------------|
| 2 | 5 cards |
| 3 | 4 cards |
| 4 | 3 cards |
| 5 | 3 cards |

**5. Build the Dungeon**
- Place the chosen Boss Mat face-up in the center of the table.
- Count out the number of Door Cards shown on the Boss Mat (e.g., 20 for Baby Barbarian).
- Separately pull 2 Challenge Cards per player from the Challenge Card pile (e.g., 6 Challenge Cards for 3 players).
- Shuffle those Door Cards and Challenge Cards together.
- Place the shuffled dungeon deck face-down on top of the Boss Mat, covering its symbols.

**Expanded / Curses! note:** If using Curses! Foiled Again!, start with the Boss Cards for the boss you are facing, add enough Door Cards to reach the total printed on the Boss Mat, then add 2 Challenge Cards per player as usual.

**6. Set the Timer**
Set your timer for 5 minutes. Do NOT start it yet.

## 7. Complete Game Flow

### Phase 1: Start the Run

1. All players draw their starting hand.
2. Start the timer.
3. Flip the top card of the dungeon pile.

### Phase 2: Defeat Cards in Real Time

There are **no turns**. All players act at once. When a Dungeon Card is flipped:

- All players look at it simultaneously.
- Players call out what they can contribute ("I have a sword!" / "I have a scroll!") — verbal communication is encouraged.
- Players physically place their cards face-up in the center of the table.
- Cards from multiple players can combine to meet the requirements.

**When all required symbols are matched:** Sweep all played cards and the Dungeon Card to the side (not into anyone's personal discard pile). Immediately flip the next Dungeon Card.

**After playing or discarding cards:** Immediately refill your hand back to your starting hand size.

### Phase 3: Handle Events

When an Event Card is flipped, its effect takes place immediately before anything else happens. See Section 18 for all event effects.

### Phase 4: Handle Mini-Bosses

Mini-Bosses work like Door Cards but require more symbols and cannot be defeated by type-specific abilities or action cards. Match all symbols to defeat it, then continue.

### Phase 5: The Boss Fight

When the entire dungeon pile is exhausted, the Boss Mat is revealed. The team must now match all the symbols shown on the Boss Mat to win. Different rules apply here — see Section 13.

### Phase 6: Victory or Defeat

**Victory:** All Boss symbols are matched → dungeon cleared → reset and proceed to next dungeon.

**Defeat:** Timer hits zero, or all players run out of cards, or the team cannot defeat a card and has no ability or action card that can help → entire run fails; return to Baby Barbarian (Boss #1) and start over.

## 8. Defeating Dungeon Cards

There are **three ways** to defeat any Door Card or Mini-Boss (with restrictions):

### Method 1: Resource Cards (Symbol Matching)

Play Resource Cards from your hand to the center of the table. Cards from different players can combine. Once all symbols on the Dungeon Card are covered, it's defeated. Cards do not need to come from the same player.

**Example:** A Door Card requires 2 Swords + 1 Arrow. Player A plays a Sword card, Player B plays a double Sword card, Player C plays an Arrow card. All 3 symbols are covered → card defeated.

### Method 2: Action Cards

Play a type-specific Action Card that matches the Dungeon Card's type:

- **Fireball** defeats any Monster
- **Backstab** defeats any Person
- **Snipe** defeats any Person
- **Mighty Leap** defeats any Obstacle
- **Sprint** defeats any Obstacle
- **Holy Hand Grenade** defeats anything (including Mini-Bosses, Events, and Bosses)

> **Important:** These do NOT work on Mini-Bosses (no type), Events, or the Boss — except for the Holy Hand Grenade, which is unrestricted.

### Method 3: Hero Special Abilities

Discard 3 cards to use your hero's ability (see Section 16). If the ability defeats a specific type (e.g., "Defeat a Monster"), it can only be used against a card of that type.

## 9. Rules for Playing Cards

### A Card Laid Is a Card Played

Once you physically place a card in the center of the table, it is played and cannot be taken back. You may show a card to teammates before playing it, but do not place it down until you intend to commit.

### Cards Go to the Center, Not Your Discard Pile

Cards played to defeat dungeon cards go to a shared "sweep" pile, not back to any player's deck. They are out of the game for that dungeon. The exception: cards discarded from your hand to use your Hero Ability go to your personal Discard Pile on your Hero Mat.

### You Can Play Non-Matching Resource Cards

During regular Door Cards (not the Boss), you may play a Resource Card that doesn't match any symbol on the current Dungeon Card. This is a strategy for cycling through your hand to find better cards. Those non-matching cards are permanently swept away (lost for the dungeon), so use this sparingly.

> **This option disappears at the Boss.** During the Boss fight, you may only play cards that match the Boss's symbols. Non-matching Resource Cards must be returned to your hand.

### Action Cards at the Boss

You also cannot play type-defeating Action Cards (Fireball, Backstab, Snipe, Mighty Leap, Sprint) against the Boss, because the Boss has no type. If you accidentally play one, return it to your hand.

## 10. Using Hero Abilities

Each hero has one Special Ability described on the bottom of their Hero Mat.

### How to Use Your Ability

1. Discard exactly 3 cards face-up to the "Discard Pile" space on your Hero Mat.
2. Announce the ability out loud to your teammates.
3. Perform the action described.

### Rules for Abilities

- You must have at least 3 cards in hand to use your ability.
- If the ability defeats a specific card type (Monster / Obstacle / Person), you can **only** use it when the active Dungeon Card is that type. You cannot use a "defeat a Monster" ability against an Obstacle.
- Abilities that do not defeat a type (e.g., draw cards, stop time) can be used at any time during the game.
- Using an ability is not the same as playing a card to the center. Therefore, **if the timer is paused, using your ability does not restart the clock.**
- Each player may only use their ability once per pause period. Once the timer is paused (by an ability), a player cannot use their ability a second time before the timer resumes.
- Cards discarded to use your ability go to **your personal Discard Pile** and can potentially be recovered by Heal or Health Potion.

---

## 11. Refilling Your Hand

After playing or discarding any cards, **immediately** refill your hand up to your starting hand size.

- If an ability or card allows you to draw extra cards first, draw those extra cards, then refill if still below hand size.
- If you somehow end up with more cards than your starting hand size (e.g., from Steal or a card draw ability), do NOT draw to refill until you fall below starting hand size through normal play.

---

## 12. Running Out of Cards

If both your hand and your draw pile are empty, you can no longer do anything. You are "dead weight" until another player helps you. Options to rescue a player with no cards:

- **Heal** (Yellow deck): One player puts their entire discard pile back on top of their draw deck.
- **Health Potion** (Yellow deck): All players recover 3 cards from their discard pile to their hand.
- **Healing Herbs** (Green deck): One player recovers 4 cards from their discard pile to hand.
- **Donate** (Purple deck): Give your whole hand to the empty player; you draw a new hand.

If ALL players simultaneously run out of cards with no way to recover, the team loses immediately.

---

## 13. The Boss Fight

When the last card of the dungeon pile is defeated (or swept away as an event), the Boss Mat is revealed.

### Boss Fight Rules

- The entire team now focuses on matching all symbols printed on the Boss Mat.
- **No non-matching Resource Cards.** Every card played must match one of the Boss's symbols. If you play a non-matching card by mistake, take it back into your hand.
- **No type-defeating Action Cards.** Fireball, Backstab, Snipe, Mighty Leap, Sprint — none of these work on the Boss. The Boss has no type.
- **Holy Hand Grenade** is the only Action Card that can instantly defeat the Boss.
- Hero Abilities that defeat types also do not work on the Boss (same reason).
- Hero Abilities that provide draws or utility (Stop Time, Animal Companion, Inspire, etc.) still work normally.
- Cards that are played toward the Boss are swept away when the Boss is defeated, just like regular Dungeon Cards.

### Boss Defeat

Once all symbols on the Boss Mat are collectively matched by the team, the dungeon is cleared. Stop the timer. Celebrate. Prepare the next dungeon.

---

## 14. Winning and Losing

### Winning

The team wins the entire game by defeating all dungeons in order, culminating in:
- **Base game:** Boss #5 — The Dungeon Master
- **Kickstarter Booster / Big Box expanded campaign:** Boss #7 — The Dungeon Master (Final Form)

You must play the bosses in numbered order. You cannot skip ahead.

### Losing

Any of the following end the dungeon run immediately:

1. **The timer reaches zero** before the boss is defeated.
2. **All players simultaneously run out of cards** (no cards in hand or draw pile for any player).
3. **An undefeatable state:** The active Dungeon Card cannot be matched, no player has a valid ability that defeats its type, and no valid Action Card is in anyone's hand. (This is an edge case — the game considers this a loss.)

On a loss, reset entirely: go back to Boss #1 (Baby Barbarian). There is no checkpoint system in the base game.

## 15. Between Dungeons

After clearing a dungeon (and before starting the next):

1. **Collect all cards.** Gather every card on the table — played cards, swept cards, discard piles, hands, draw piles.
2. **Sort by card back.** Each color goes back to its owner. Challenge Cards and Door Cards go back to the shared pool.
3. **Each player shuffles their full deck** (hand + draw pile + discard pile combined into one fresh deck).
4. **Optional:** Swap heroes. Between dungeons, players may choose a different hero (using a different color deck). New players can join; existing players can leave.
5. **Build the new dungeon.** Place the next Boss Mat, count out the number of Door Cards it specifies, add 2 Challenge Cards per player, shuffle, and place on the Boss Mat.
6. **Draw starting hands** again (same table as before based on player count).
7. **Reset timer to 5 minutes.**
8. **Start timer** on first card flip.

## 16. All Heroes — Abilities, Decks & Unique Cards

Each Hero Mat is double-sided. Both sides of a mat share the same physical card deck (same color, same cards). However, each hero on a mat has a different Special Ability.

---

### 🔵 Blue Deck — Wizard & Sorceress

**Deck Specialty:** Most Scroll symbols. Best choice when scrolls are needed.

**Wizard — Special Ability: Stop Time**
Discard 3 cards → Pause the game timer. The timer stays paused until any player plays a card from their hand to the center of the table. Discarding, using abilities, or flipping a new Dungeon Card does NOT restart the clock. This is the most uniquely powerful ability in the game — it gives the whole team free thinking time.

**Sorceress — Special Ability: Teleport**
Discard 3 cards → Instantly defeat the current Obstacle Dungeon Card (type must be Obstacle).

**Unique Blue Deck Action Cards:**

| Card | Effect |
|------|--------|
| **Fireball** | Instantly defeat a Monster Door Card |
| **Magic Bomb** | Counts as 1 of every symbol (Sword, Arrow, Scroll, Jump, Shield) when played. You only use the symbols needed — leftover symbols are simply unused. Extremely valuable against Bosses or cards requiring many different symbols. |
| **Cancel** | When an Event Card is flipped, play Cancel to stop the event from taking effect before it happens. The event is then swept away as if it had been completed. |

**Deck Specialty:** Most Shield symbols. Best choice when shields are needed.

**Paladin — Special Ability: Smite**
Discard 3 cards → Instantly defeat the current Monster Door Card (type must be Monster).

**Valkyrie — Special Ability: Inspire**
Discard 3 cards → All OTHER players (not you) each draw 2 cards. Excellent in 3–5 player games where teammates may be running low.

## 18. Challenge Cards

Challenge Cards have a **horned skull back** and are shuffled into the dungeon in quantities of 2 per player, unless a specific expanded boss changes the setup. They are either **Mini-Bosses** or **Events**.

Mini-Bosses have no Monster / Obstacle / Person type. They cannot be defeated by Hero Abilities or type-specific Action Cards. Only symbol matching or the Holy Hand Grenade works.

Events resolve immediately. Cancel or Holy Hand Grenade can stop an Event before it takes effect.

### 18a. Base Game Challenge Cards — Mini-Bosses (5)

## 20. Type-Defeating Action Cards by Deck

Action Cards are tied to specific Hero Decks. Do not treat them as universal unless your physical edition or an expansion card explicitly says otherwise.

| Card | Defeats / Effect | Deck |
|------|------------------|------|
| **Fireball** | Defeat a Monster | Blue — Wizard / Sorceress |
| **Mighty Leap** | Defeat an Obstacle | Red — Barbarian / Gladiator |
| **Sprint** | Defeat an Obstacle | Purple — Ninja / Thief |
| **Backstab** | Defeat a Person | Purple — Ninja / Thief |
| **Snipe** | Defeat a Person | Green — Ranger / Huntress |
| **Holy Hand Grenade** | Defeat any current card, including Mini-Bosses, Events, and Bosses | Yellow — Paladin / Valkyrie |

### Important Restrictions

- Fireball, Mighty Leap, Sprint, Backstab, and Snipe do **not** defeat Mini-Bosses, Events, or Bosses because those cards do not have the matching Monster / Obstacle / Person type.
- Holy Hand Grenade is the exception: it can defeat anything, including the Boss.
- Curses! Foiled Again! adds new Action Cards to the original decks, but those should be treated as expansion content, not base-game universal cards.

## 21. Key Rules Edge Cases & Clarifications

**Q: Can I use my Hero Ability while the timer is paused?**
A: Yes. Using an ability does not restart the timer. However, each player may only use their ability once per pause period.

**Q: Does flipping a new Door Card restart the timer after a Stop Time / Divine Shield pause?**
A: No. The timer remains paused until an actual card is physically played to the center of the table.

**Q: If I use Donate and give my hand to a teammate, what is my new hand size?**
A: Draw immediately up to your starting hand size.

**Q: Can I play a Wild Card "as a Sword" toward a Boss that needs 3 Swords?**
A: Yes. Declare it as a Sword when you play it. It counts for one of the 3 required Swords.

**Q: The Holy Hand Grenade was played against a Boss — does the dungeon end immediately?**
A: Yes. The Holy Hand Grenade defeats any card outright, including the Boss. The dungeon is cleared instantly.

**Q: We flipped an Event Card — can we play resource cards while dealing with it?**
A: No. The event must be resolved before play continues. Only Cancel or Holy Hand Grenade can prevent it.

**Q: We're facing a Mini-Boss. Can the Paladin use Smite?**
A: No. Mini-Bosses have no type. Smite only works on Monsters.

**Q: Can we talk about our cards?**
A: Yes, completely. You can show cards, discuss strategy, call out what you have. The game rewards communication. The only restriction is time — you're racing the clock.

**Q: If Confusion is played and the timer is paused, is passing hands considered "playing a card"?**
A: No — passing is resolving the event, not playing to the center. Timer stays paused.

**Q: Can two players both play cards toward the same Dungeon Card simultaneously?**
A: Yes, and this is the intended play. There are no turn restrictions. Multiple players can dump cards simultaneously.

**Q: What happens if I have 5 cards in hand and use Steal to take another player's 4-card hand, giving me 9 cards?**
A: You keep all 9 cards. Do not refill. When you play/discard enough cards to drop below your starting hand size, you begin refilling again normally.

## 22. Strategy Guide

### Team Composition

A well-rounded party covers all five symbol types and includes at least one of each category:

- **Time control:** Wizard (Stop Time) is almost always valuable. It lets the team regroup without losing time.
- **Healing/recovery:** Yellow deck (Paladin/Valkyrie) is near-essential at higher player counts. Heal, Health Potion, and Holy Hand Grenade are game-changing.
- **Broad draw:** Huntress (Animal Companion) or Thief (Pickpocket) ensure no player runs dry.
- **Type-killing coverage:** Ideally the team can handle all three types (Monster, Obstacle, Person) without always needing to match symbols. Paladin/Barbarian for Monsters, Sorceress/Ninja for Obstacles, Ranger/Gladiator for Persons.

### General Tips

- **Communicate constantly.** Call out what you have before slamming it down — avoid accidentally covering a symbol someone else already covered with a better card.
- **Don't dump non-matching cards carelessly.** Each Resource Card played to the center is gone for the rest of the dungeon. Cycling aggressively can leave you empty at the worst time.
- **Save the Holy Hand Grenade.** It's the only card that can instantly win the Boss fight. Unless you're in a crisis mid-dungeon, hold onto it.
- **Use Stop Time when you hit a tough card.** A Mini-Boss, a complex event, or a card you don't have coverage for are all good times to burn the Wizard ability.
- **Health Potion after Sudden Illness.** If Sudden Illness forces everyone to discard, immediately play Health Potion (or Healing Herbs) to recover key cards before continuing.
- **Two-player note:** In 2-player games, use the full merged deck but remember your effective "draw pile" is much deeper — you have access to more card types, which compensates for the smaller hand size.
- **Boss symbol planning:** Before the dungeon starts, glance at the Boss's symbols and mentally assign "who is responsible for which symbol." This helps avoid the Boss being a last-minute scramble.
- **The last few cards before the Boss matter most.** Don't burn all your Shield cards on Door Cards if the Boss requires 3 Shields — leave some reserve for the final fight.

## 23. Verification Standard & Source Confidence

This guide is written as a practical play reference, not as a replacement for the cards in your box. Use this standard when resolving conflicts:

1. **Your physical cards and mats win.** If a card/mat in your edition says something different, use the printed component.
2. **Official rules win for procedures.** Setup, timing, refilling, Challenge Card handling, Boss Card/Curses/Artifact procedures, and expansion setup should follow the official rulebooks.
3. **Public card databases are used for lookup details.** Boss symbols, individual Challenge Card names, and many expanded card effects are compiled from public wiki/card-list sources. These are useful but can lag behind editions or contain transcription errors.
4. **Expanded/Kickstarter/Big Box material is edition-sensitive.** Treat those sections as reference checklists unless you verify them against your own copy.
